Principles

1. Aufbau Principle

Lower energy levels must be filled completely before moving to higher ones.

1s < 2s < 2p < 3s < 3p < 4s < 3d < 4p
2. Pauli Exclusion Principle

An orbital can hold a maximum of 2 electrons, which must have opposite spins (up and down).

3. Hund's Rule

Electrons occupy empty orbitals of the same energy singly with parallel spins first.

Orbitals per subshell:
s = 1 | p = 3 | d = 5
4. Stability Exceptions

Chromium (Z=24) and Copper (Z=29) promote a 4s electron to 3d to achieve more stable half-filled or fully-filled d-subshells.
