Why are Ionic Crystals Brittle?

In an ionic lattice, ions are held together by strong electrostatic attractions between opposite charges. Watch what happens to the internal structure when a physical force is applied.

🔨

REPULSION!

Like charges align and push apart.

Teacher's Note:

  • Initial State: Cations (+) and Anions (–) alternating, there are strong non-directional electrostatic force between cations and anions (shown by green arrows).
  • Force Applied: The stress shifts one layer of ions by just one position.
  • Brittleness: Due to this relative motion, ions of the same charge suddenly line up. The resulting electrostatic repulsion (shown by red arrows) splits the crystal along a cleavage plane.
