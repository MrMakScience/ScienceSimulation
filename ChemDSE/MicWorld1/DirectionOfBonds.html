Ionic Bond (Non-Directional)

The \( Na^+ \) ion is attracted by \( Cl^- \) ions and repelled by \( Na^+ \) ions in all directions equally. There is no specific "axis" for the force.

Summary for Students

  • Covalent: Directional. The nuclei are pulled specifically toward the center where the electrons are.
  • Ionic: Non-directional. Forces act like a field, pulling or pushing in every direction around the ion.
